# Eligibility Decision Framework

## Overview

The Lersha Credit Scoring System produces a final eligibility classification for each farmer: **Eligible**, **Review**, or **Not Eligible**. This classification is generated by an ensemble of machine learning models (XGBoost, Random Forest, CatBoost) trained on the 20 Data Point scores and 34 derived features. The models learn complex patterns in the data that go beyond simple threshold-based rules, capturing interactions between features that determine creditworthiness.

## The Three Decision Outcomes

### Eligible
The farmer demonstrates strong creditworthiness indicators across multiple dimensions. Key characteristics of Eligible farmers typically include:
- Positive net income (total income exceeds total costs)
- Diversified income sources (crop + livestock + off-farm)
- Formal land documentation (land title available)
- Low or no outstanding debt
- Strong social capital (cooperative membership, community engagement)
- Adequate adaptive capacity (water access, insurance, institutional ties)

**Lending recommendation**: Approved for credit at the requested amount, subject to standard lending terms.

### Review
The farmer presents a borderline profile with mixed indicators — some dimensions are favorable while others raise concerns. Common Review profiles include:
- Adequate income but high existing debt
- Strong production potential but elevated climate risk
- Good social capital but limited formal documentation
- First-time borrowers with no credit history but strong fundamentals

**Lending recommendation**: Requires manual assessment by a loan officer. The officer should examine the specific weak areas identified by SHAP feature importances and determine whether they can be mitigated through loan structuring (smaller amount, different repayment schedule, collateral requirements).

### Not Eligible
The farmer does not currently meet creditworthiness requirements. This does not mean the farmer is permanently excluded — it means the current assessment identifies significant risk factors that need to be addressed before credit can be extended. Typical Not Eligible profiles include:
- Negative or very low net income
- Heavy existing debt burden
- No land title and limited assets
- High climate exposure with low adaptive capacity
- Limited institutional engagement and social capital

**Lending recommendation**: Not recommended for loan approval at this time. The farmer should be provided with specific guidance on which factors drove the decision and how they might improve their score (e.g., obtaining land certification, joining a cooperative, diversifying income).

## How the Decision Is Made

### Step 1: Data Collection
A field agent conducts the 50-question survey with the farmer, collecting demographic, agronomic, financial, credit, climate, and adaptive capacity data.

### Step 2: Data Point Calculation
The 20 Data Points (DP1–DP20) are calculated from survey responses and CropRef external data, each calculated from survey responses and external data.

### Step 3: Feature Engineering
The 34 ML model features are derived from the survey data, including calculated fields like net_income, yield_per_hectare, input_intensity, and income_per_family_member.

### Step 4: ML Ensemble Prediction
Three machine learning models independently classify the farmer:
- **XGBoost**: Gradient-boosted decision trees optimized for tabular data
- **Random Forest**: Ensemble of decision trees with bagging for variance reduction
- **CatBoost**: Gradient boosting with native categorical feature handling

The ensemble combines these predictions to produce the final classification, reducing individual model bias and improving prediction stability.

### Step 5: SHAP Explainability
SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values are calculated for each prediction, quantifying how much each feature contributed to pushing the prediction toward or away from each class. The top contributing features are identified for explanation generation.

### Step 6: RAG Explanation
The prediction and SHAP values are used to retrieve relevant knowledge base documents, which are then combined with the farmer's specific data to generate a natural-language explanation using Google Gemini.

## Prediction Confidence

### Class Probabilities
Each ML model outputs class probabilities via `predict_proba()` for all three eligibility classes. For example, a prediction might produce:
- Eligible: 0.78, Review: 0.15, Not Eligible: 0.07

The class with the highest probability becomes the predicted classification. The confidence score equals the highest probability value (0.78 in this example), indicating how certain the model is in its prediction.

### Interpreting Confidence Scores
- **High confidence (>0.70)**: The model strongly favors one class — the farmer's profile clearly aligns with one eligibility category
- **Moderate confidence (0.50–0.70)**: The model leans toward one class but sees some characteristics of other classes — common in borderline Review cases
- **Low confidence (<0.50)**: The model is uncertain — the farmer's profile has mixed signals that don't clearly fit any single category

### Feature Importance in Decisions

Based on the model's learned patterns, the features that typically have the strongest influence on the eligibility decision include:

**Strongest positive indicators (pushing toward Eligible)**:
- High net_income — demonstrates clear repayment capacity
- Large own_farmland_size — indicates asset wealth and production potential
- High yield_per_hectare — signals productive farming practices
- Land title available — provides collateral security
- Low family_size relative to income — good per-capita welfare
- High institutional_support_score — strong engagement with support systems

**Strongest negative indicators (pushing toward Not Eligible)**:
- Negative or very low net_income — insufficient repayment capacity
- Large family_size with low income — high dependency burden
- No land title — limited collateral and tenure security
- High outstanding loan amount — existing debt burden
- No water_reserve_access in drought-prone area — high climate risk
- Low institutional_support_score — limited support network

## Sample Decision Profiles

### Profile A: Eligible Farmer
- Female, age 45, 3 family members

- 2.5 ha owned land with title, grows wheat (2 ha) + chickpea (0.5 ha)
- Net income: 65,000 ETB, no outstanding loans
- Has irrigation access and crop insurance
- **Score drivers**: High net income, land title, diversified income, strong adaptive capacity

### Profile B: Review Farmer
- Male, age 30, 6 family members
- Traditional house with multiple rooms, Edir member
- 1.5 ha family land (no title), grows maize (1.5 ha)
- Net income: 28,000 ETB, outstanding loan 8,000 ETB
- No irrigation, uses traditional tools
- **Score drivers**: Positive income but existing debt, no land title, limited adaptive capacity, young age with less experience

### Profile C: Not Eligible Farmer
- Male, age 22, 8 family members
- Traditional all-in-one house, no institutional memberships
- 0.5 ha rented land (no title), grows teff (0.5 ha)
- Net income: 5,200 ETB, outstanding loan 15,000 ETB
- No off-farm income, no irrigation, manual tools only
- **Score drivers**: Very low income, heavy debt, large household, no assets, no institutional support, high climate vulnerability

## Feedback Loop

The credit decision is not final — it serves as input to a continuous improvement cycle:
1. Farmers classified as "Not Eligible" receive guidance on improving their profile
2. Farmers who receive loans are tracked for repayment behavior
3. Repayment outcomes feed back into model retraining
4. The CropRef table is updated annually with current market prices and agronomic data
5. Survey questions and scoring weights are refined based on predictive performance
